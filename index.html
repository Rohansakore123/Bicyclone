Motivation

 The ratio of population to motor vehicles in most cities of India has reached unsustainable levels resulting in clogged traffic, air pollution, and reduced productivity. Bicycle share is self motivated project motivated to ensure cities can continue to grow without choking on infrastructure bottlenecks.

Design Brief

 In india due to explosion of population number of vehicals on road has increased considerably which is one of the major reason for problems of transport and health issues . A bicycle share system which is specially designed for user needs of India is a network of bicycles and stations managed automatically underhuman supervison that allows user to make short trips conviniently and affordably Also a handheld application which allows users to search for nearby bike docking station and rent or share the bike.

Literature Review

 There are 495 similar operating programs for bike share in the world while India has only 7 of them despite of the huge population. Annual growth rate of such start-ups is 14% and more than 500,000 bicycles are currently deployed. With the increasing air pollution which affects health bicycle is an eco-friendly and healthy alternative

  • Young adults who are using bikes are 48% less likely to be overweight.
  • 3hrs of riding per week reduces risk of heart disease by 50%.
  • 29% of bike users reduced their automobile use in america.
  • India has Fourteen in 20 most polluted cities in the world according to WHO (World Health Organization)

Interestingly, in India, cycling isn’t new as a mode of transportation. From children riding bicycles to schools, to the local milkman, cycles are widely used. However, with the push towards motorisation, the number of bicycles on the road have dropped significantly. Now, with the launch of smart bikes or bike-sharing, commuters will have an alternative mode for last mile commute, which would also control pollution.

Interviews

It was necessary to begin with the real human needs that made up this complex humanitarian crisis, so I decided to interview few people to get an understanding of the problem and needs for bicycle share service in India . About 6 candidates were interviewed who had at least some knowledge of smartphones and are willing to use bicycle for covering smaller distances. We seperated our users based on three main categories based on their usage of shared bikes. The user in earch category seek different goals and features.
